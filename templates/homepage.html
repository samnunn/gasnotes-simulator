Simulation Basics

Simulation is a way to practice. You can use practice to build strengths and find weaknesses in your self, your team, and your system without all the headaches of putting patients in harm’s way.

No athlete would hit the pitch without practice, and neither should you.

Scenario Design

Simulation is a means to an end, so begin with the end in mind. Whether it's hitting learning outcomes or meeting KPIs, every element of your sim should be evaluated in light of what you set out to achieve.

Common ingredients of a simulation plan are:

  • An ISOBAR summary of your patient's status
  • An opening handover script (or call for help)
  • Three or four phases of deterioration and recovery

Be ready for your plan and your learning outcomes to fly out the window.

The Pre-Breif

Most newcomers to sim are nervous. This is natural. The worst thing you can do is bring their fears to life by embarrassing them or tricking them into failure. Nothing will be learned, and nobody will come to your next sim.

Your mission is to suspend their anxiety and transition to a parallel universe where risk-free experimentation is welcome and wanted. You should start by internalising and then preaching the Basic Assumption…

We believe that everyone participating in activities using Gas Notes tools is intelligent, capable, cares about doing their best, and wants to improve.

Adapted from Center for Medical Simulation, Boston, Massachusetts, USA.
